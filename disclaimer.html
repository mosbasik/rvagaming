Clarity Disclaimer

If you are seeing this page, it's probably because I've linked you to it from something I've written.

I enjoy writing, and I enjoy organizing my thoughts and laying them out well for easy reading. Clarity is the single most important characteristic aspect of a piece written for the eyes of others. Unfortunately, clarity is woefully lacking in most of the material produced by pundits, critics, politicists, armchair philosophers, and trolls I see on the Internet.

That being said, keep in mind that quite often I'm not writing for you. I'm writing for me - to read it later, to remember it, to get it out of my head, whatever. And quite often I don't have the time to organize everything to my satisfaction before I hit "submit" - sometimes I'm just lucky to have five minutes to jot down a few thoughts. If I have more time, I'll produce more content. I probably won't spend that time staring at what I've already written trying to predict how others would misinterpret it. I do enough of that while writing university papers.

This also means that if you confront me about something I've written, I might not have thought about it from whatever perspective you present. I reserve the right, then, to retract, modify or condition any or all of my statements if I so choose.

Now, read.
